Handmade™ — A Quick Guide for You 🧶

This is the first version of the knitting app. It's rough and early, so don't worry about breaking anything. I mostly want to know what feels good and what doesn't — play with it like a sketch, not a finished thing.

What this app is for

The purpose: to be the single, calm home for your knitting — so your patterns, your projects, your swatches, and your gauge all live in one place instead of scattered across notebooks, photos, and apps. The big idea that makes it different from other knitting apps: over time it should learn your gauge and tell you which needle to reach for with a given yarn.

What it does right now (this version)

  • Keep your projects in one place, each with a photo and your swatch details.
  • Save a swatch when you start a project — gauge (stitches & rows per 10 cm), yarn, and needle size.
  • Browse a pattern library and filter it by category, needle size, or yarn type.
  • A first taste of gauge lookup — type a yarn and see which needle gave you the right gauge last time.

What's coming next (planned soon)

  • A proper row/stitch counter that doesn't lose your place.
  • Editing and linking — tie a project to a pattern, tidy things up as you go.
  • A fuller pattern library with your own patterns front and centre.

Where it's heading (later)

  • Real gauge learning — not just remembering, but estimating the right needle for a new yarn based on how you knit.
  • "Patterns you may also like" suggestions.
  • Eventually a polished app from the App Store, with a free version and an optional paid one.

This version exists so you can react to the idea early and help me decide what to build first.

Getting it on your phone

  1. Open this link in Safari on your iPhone:
    Open Handmade™
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with the arrow pointing up).
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen."
  4. Tap Add. Now there's a Handmade icon on your home screen, just like a real app. Open it from there.
Everything you enter is saved on your phone only. Nothing is shared anywhere. (Which also means it won't appear on your iPad — just this phone, for now.)

What you can do right now

There are three tabs along the bottom: Projects, Patterns, and Gauge.

🧶 Projects — start a make and save your swatch

  • Tap Projects → + New.
  • Give it a name (e.g. "Autumn Cardigan").
  • Take a photo of your swatch (or pick one from your library).
  • Fill in your gauge — stitches and rows per 10 cm.
  • Add the yarn you used and the needle size.
  • Tap Save Project.

It'll appear in your Projects list. Tap it to see its details, edit it, or delete it.

📐 Patterns — find the right pattern fast

There are a few example patterns in here already. Try the filters at the top — by category (Sweater, Hat, Shawl…), by needle size, or by yarn type. You can also tap + Add to put in one of your own patterns.

📏 Gauge — "what needle did I use last time?"

This is the idea I'm most curious about. Type in a yarn name, and the app shows you which needle gave you the right gauge the last time you used that yarn (from the swatches you've saved). It only works once you've saved a project or two with that yarn — so it'll feel empty at first. That's expected. The more you use it, the smarter it gets.
